Neumark Survey of 1718-19 (Königsberg District of Prussia)

historical background of the colonisation

Since the mid-17th Century, the population of Brandenburg had been steadily diminished not only by the Plague but also by various military conflicts, including the Thirty Years War. However, under the reign of Prince-Elector Frederick III (1688-1713, King of Prussia from 1701) Brandenburg's population increased by about a third. Central Europe was an area of frequent territorial gains and losses, thus Frederick had many political and economic reasons to continue the policy of Eastern Colonisation. In 1718, he ordered a survey and unlike other German historical Censuses, this one recorded family names and professions, quite obviously for the purpose of tax collection.

The following agricultural personal descriptions are listed in the dataset:

Bauer = land-owning farmer with several farm buildings - often employs some labourers/maids

Kossäth = owns cottage & small amount of land - often had to supplement income through day-labouring

Freihäusler = owns cottage - free to use the premises e.g. to brew beer, works on squire's land and pays tax

This only a fraction of the large amount of data processed for this project. German historian Paul Schwartz compiled the original 1718-19 survey into a three-volume publication.
